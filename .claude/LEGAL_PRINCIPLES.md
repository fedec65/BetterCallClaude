### 4. Precedent System (BGE / ATF / DTF)

**Nature of Precedents**:
- BGE = persuasive authority (not binding like common law)
- Bundesgericht strives for consistency
- Lower courts generally follow BGE
- Departure requires strong justification

**Precedent Analysis Framework**:
1. **Identify ratio decidendi** - Core legal principle
2. **Distinguish facts** - Material differences from current case
3. **Consider evolution** - Newer BGE may modify older principles
4. **Assess persuasiveness** - Chamber, vote split, reasoning quality
5. **Check overruling** - Later BGE explicitly departing

### 5. Burden of Proof (Beweislast / fardeau de la preuve / onere della prova)

**General Rule** (Art. 8 ZGB / CC / CC):

- Each party proves facts favorable to their position

**Standard of Proof**:

## üåç Multi-Jurisdictional Considerations

### Federal vs. Cantonal Law Analysis

**Step 1: Jurisdiction Determination**
- Is this federal competence? (Art. 42-135 BV)
- Or cantonal residual competence? (Art. 3 BV)
- Concurrent jurisdiction?

**Step 2: Applicable Law**
- Federal statute exists ‚Üí federal law applies
- Federal statute with cantonal execution ‚Üí consider both
- No federal statute ‚Üí cantonal law applies

**Step 3: Procedural Law**
- Civil procedure: ZPO (federal) or cantonal ZPO
- Criminal procedure: StPO (federal)
- Administrative procedure: VwVG (federal) or cantonal laws

**Step 4: Conflict Resolution**
- Federal law supremacy (Art. 49 BV)
- Cantonal law remains valid if not contradictory
- Harmonious interpretation preferred
